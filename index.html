Named

after a religious figure who lived there, Sidi Bou Said is an attractive village located about 20 km from the capital Tunis, known for its extensive use of blue and white.

The entire town

is located on top of a steep cliff, which overlooks the Mediterranean Sea. The view from Sidi Bou Said is absolutely phenomenal, and will continue to amaze you throughout your entire trip.

Simply beautiful,

with flowers draped from almost every wall and doorway, and beautiful flowing vines hanging throughout the entire area. Much of the town is quiet, and inhabited mostly by locals going about their daily business.

The streets,

most of which are quaint cobbled streets, are lined for the most part with souvenir shops, cafe’s, and art shops. The entire town has an intense feel of artistry and craftsmanship, and the locals are very proud of their town, and don’t hesitate to show it off.

You'll never guess

the beauties hidden behind the nail-studded doors...

Elegantly

decorated houses, waiting to be discovered.

Do not miss

one of the most beautiful of them: the Palace of Baron d'Erlanger...

A banker and above all an artist,

who succumbed to the village beauty like Paul Klee, August Macke and André Gide before him...

Admire

the many views this place offers...

And if tired,

have a rest and a cup of tea or coffee with a panoramic view over the bay..

or

just in the cosy coffee shop on top ofthe hill.

At night,

on your way home, catch the magical atmosphere of sunset colors, jasmin frangrances and call of birds going to sleep....
